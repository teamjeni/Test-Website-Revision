Stone Pricing

TURQUOISE

Or turquoise cabochons are priced from $1.50/ct to $4.00/ct based upon hardness, color, webbing, matrix and pitting. Pitting is common in turquoise and variscite, so it has the least bearing on the price if it is minor and has little or no effect on the stones beauty. Color is a very subjective matter so it plays only a minor role. Hardness is the most important factor. The hardest turquoise is rated 6.0 on Mohs scale and takes an exceptional polish. Turquoise can also be a little softer and take a great polish. Turquoise, which is found close to the top of the deposit, can be oxidized and quite soft and is called chalk. Even the hardest chalk will not take a good polish and it is generally infused with epoxy to make it harder. Stabilized turquoise is less valuable than natural turquoise.

To arrive at a per carat price on our natural stone we first consider the hardness. The stone must be hard enough to take a good to fantastic shine and we rate it as good, better, and best -- this is generally a reflection of the hardness. We also monitor hardness while cutting. We then consider the matrix and/or spider webbing if present and consider its beauty. In the case of matrix, we ask ourselves if it adds additional character to the stone and judge if it is has a good balance with the turquoise. Even stones with more matrix than turquoise can be attractive, but we don't feel that we can charge as much for stones with an abundance of matrix. We then consider other factors such as minor pitting or other minor irregularities and even the stones thickness. We sometimes cut thin, but attractive stones, that are discounted because they require a higher backing to stone ratio.

We also offer ribbon turquoise, which is a solid ribbon or turquoise vein running through the the rock matrix, and boulder cut turquoise, which is a veneer of turquoise over host rock, at reduced prices.

VARISCITE

For the most part variscite undergoes the same consideration as turquoise for pricing. All of our variscite is hard, harder in fact than variscite is generally considered to be. This is due to the presence of silica which is common for Nevada variscite. Therefore, the price is based upon the matrix balance and pitting, if present, and ranges from $1.00/ct - $1.50/ct. Some exceptional or rare stones will be priced at $1.75/ct.

TREATMENT/STABILIZATION

All of our stones are backed with Epoxy. This is the standard for turquoise and variscite cabochons. It helps with setting the stone and it can be sanded to better help accommodate variable bezel sizes.

In general all of our stones are natural, meaning no enhancements or treatments have been applied to the stones. This is always clearly marked. However, from time to time we may offer stabilized stones at a reduced price. This too will be clearly marked.
